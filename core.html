CORE

Currently the market is saturated with cloud based services targeting a wide range audience. Core is a cloud based storage website and mobile application which places the power and control in the users hands while making the experience easy and affordable.

VIEW INVISION PROTOTYPE

What is CORE?

Core is a cloud based storage service with value placed on usability. Core’s efficiency allows the user to organize and manage content while collaborating with others. Core offers storage, content creation, real-time collaboration and visual organization.

The Challenge

There are several cloud based services currently accessible for personal and business use. Products such as iCloud, Dropbox, Google Drive and Pinterest seek to find the balance between the user’s needs and wants. With this in mind, the challenge was to create a solution that eliminated frustrations and enabled better satisfaction over the current available options.

Competitive Analysis

A competitive Analysis was completed to gather and understand the current market and user flows were created for each company analyzed. The analysis gave me a better understanding of the potential gaps as well as untapped opportunities within the market and any room for improvement. From a design perspective the biggest takeaway was intuitiveness and an ability to collaborate. The companies analyzed were Dropbox, Pinterest, Google Drive and iCloud. Below are the SWOT anaylsis from two of the top competitors.

Google Drive SWOT Analysis

Strengths:

  • Intuitive for the user
  • Organizational features are built in and easy to use.
  • Can be accessed through any platform such as Mac, PC, tablet, or smartphone.
  • Allows for easy sharing of documents between multipe persons
  • Ease of interface with the different features such as google documents, drive, calendar, slides and classroom.
  • Work is automatically saved.
  • Free storage up to 15GB.
  • A sharable link is available whenever the user needs to share something whether it is with one or many.

Weaknesses:

  • Must have a gmail account.
  • Documents that have been shared remain shared unless the status is changed.
  • Requires access to the internet.

Opportunities:

  • Documents can be saved in one location and accessed by several people.
  • Collaberation can happen in any setting physically or virtually.
  • Presentations can be shared physically or virtually this includes the use of slides, text and videos.
  • An App can be downloaded and used on personal devices such as tablet or smartphone.
  • Access to google search engines as well as images.

Threats:

  • The user must always have a reliable internet connection.
  • Occasional gliches when downloading or uploading documents, presentations, and images.
  • Shared documents between persons/groups remain shared unless creator revokes rights.
  • Sometimes documents do not saves correctly when going from a windows based document to google docs.
  • Links will sometimes break which renders the link useless.

Summary:

Overall, Google Drive offers several options for the average user which are free up to a certain point. Since most people and even colleges utilize gmail as their preferred email using google drive as an automatic and easy solution for storing and sharing is seamless and easy for any user.

Dropbox SWOT Analysis

Strengths:

  • Intuitive for the user.
  • There is a high user based of over 400 million users.
  • Easy installation process - but not as accessible as Google Drive which you receive access to when establishing an email account with Google.
  • Allows for easy sharing of documents between multiple persons.
  • High awareness among users as well as internet users.
  • Innovative because it replaced the USB drive.
  • 1TB of free storage before payment plan is necessary.

Weaknesses

  • Smaller amount of free storage unlike Google Drive.
  • Security - cloud based servers is a concern for users.
  • Requires access to the internet.

Oportunities

  • Document can be saved in one location and accessed by several people.
  • Collaberation can happen in any setting physically or virtually.
  • Presentations can be shared physically or virtually this includes the use of slides, text and videos.
  • The App can be downloaded and used on personal devices such as an tablet or smartphone.
  • Access to Google search engines as well as images.

Threats

  • Intense competitors in Google Drive, Skydrive and SugarSync
  • Changes in government regulations regarding server locations.
  • Security and privacy issues.
  • Constant updating of the platform so it does not become obsolete.
  • Better products.

Summary:

Dropbox seems to be at a competative state with Google Drive. There are some slight disadvantages from Google drive, but companies on a whole seem impressed and use this technology. Furthermore, Dropbox has the users to back up the platform.

User Research

This began wtih a survey that was sent out to social media contacts, friends, and peers to understand the users, the users needs and the users wants. The survey focused on the following:

  • The specific cloud storage app/platform currently used.
  • What do they specifically use the app/platform for - i.e. storage of photos, collaboration with others, or personal and professional use.
  • What features about the app/platform is most valubale?
  • What features about the app/platform doe they value the least?
  • What features or capabilities would they values if added onto the app/platform?
  • What are their current frustrations with the app/platform?
  • What are the current tasks for which they use the app/platform?

Surveying almost twenty people, the data showed that 94% used cloud storage on a regular basis and over half use cloud storage on a daily basis. And of the 16 people surveyed, 66% provided feedback/recommendations on how to improve cloud storage.

Research showed that the frustration with cloud storage has to do with intuitiveness of the product. The user wants the ability to interface with the product easily with the option of guided help or live support through phone or chat. Finally, the research showed that the cost of the service must be competitive in price with options for both the professional, personal or student.

The Key Points:

  • Collaboration tools across multiple platforms
  • Organization of data
  • Ability to save photos, documents, and music across platforms into one location.

User Personas

Understanding the user is the most important part of the process, after asking in-depth questions the following two personas emerged.

Susan

Age: 54

Seattle, Washington

College Professor

"Teaching is my passion."

Biography

Susan is married with two grown children. She is a professor who teaches design thinking. Susan loves to assign work that draws her students together as team members. By forcing them to collaberate on assignments, she is teaching them about working in teams. Susan is able to view the progress of the teams via the cloud storage system where her students are required to keep and share project information and data.

Motivation

Susan loves being a teacher. It is both her passion and profession; however, being able to track her students projects online, see who is contributing versus who is not is a must for any type of software and cloud storage that she uses. There are also times that she needs her students to not only view large documents and files that contain both videos and images, therefore finding a cloud storage service that will allow a lot of data for little to no cost is a huge plus.

Frustrations

  • Organization of files & data
  • Collaboration
  • Storage of data

Goals

  • Keeping track of student collaboration and progress on projects.
  • Ability to view projects/data anywhere
  • Keep costs low and affordable for students

Daniel

Age: 20

Chicago, IL

Student/Outdoor enthusiast

"I am inspired by collaborating with others."

Biography

Daniel is a college student studying graphic design and frontend development. He attends college in person, but is required to collaborate with other fellow students on various assignments. He spends several hours a day online either creating or editing his designs while collaborting with his peers. As much as he enjoys collaboration it would be easier if the files were updated simultaneously.

Motivation

Daniel find inspiration in the collaboration with other. Over the past three yaers he hast spend countless hours studying, designing, creating, and collaboration with others; however, cloud storage is an issue. Daniel would like to finda way to incorporate more user friendly beneifts into his everyday use of cloud storage. Perhaps files be automatically synced from each devices and stored.

Frustrations

  • Ability to share via social media
  • Simultaneous updates of documents and tracking for these updates.
  • Security of shared data.

Goals

  • Community enhanced by collaboration.
  • Organization of files, images, and videos for projects.
  • Storage of data accessible through multiple platforms.

User Stories

High Priorties:

  • Seamless saving of an item with auto save.
  • Ability to save a link in social media.
  • Auto uploading of files when initially created.
  • Allow for initial organization preferences in settings under ctegories, tags, groups and/or folders.
  • Local file system syncing.
  • Digial App for smartphone and tablet.
  • Seamless saving of data images, videos, and links.
  • Handles large file transfers.
  • Ability to create notes, documents, and spreadsheets within cloud.
  • After documents, notes and spreadsheets with cloud.
  • Intuitive software.

Medium Priorities:

  • Save content while collaborating within the file.
  • Ability to share content via sharable link on social media or within email.
  • Supports an active directory where the user can easily see what files/folders/groups are in their cloud.
  • Local file & version recovery.
  • Saving content frome ails with the ability to track changes to a word document, notes, spreadsheets, and Powerpoint.
  • Group permissions.
  • Encryption of files when activated by the user for shared content.
  • Two step verification.
  • Single sign on.

Low Priorities:

  • Drag and drop files.
  • Saving links from emails.
  • Open collaboration folders with ease.
  • Allow for automatic uploading to desired files.
  • Short videos that show how to create content.
  • Short videos on how to correctly use software.

Brand & Identity

This is where ideas and concepts are more than a thought or idea. Based on research, the brand needed to conceptualize the place were valued items are stored and shared, almost like a computer hard-drive. Based on research the brand needed to be trustworthy and simplistic.

The final logo was user tested to ensure both readability and brand identity.

Typography

Type is branding. Helvetica has a strong history within the design community and it was chosen to suppor the brand and identity of Core because of its openness, neutrality and symmetry.

Color

There is a high contrast between dark gray, light gray and white. This allows for better readability. The primary color is color is blue to emphasize trust and loyalty. While the purple color was chosen because it positively affects the user’s attention and flow.
